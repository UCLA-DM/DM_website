Research Directions

The research of our lab is centered around graphs -- the ubiquitous data structure that can represent data, knowledge, computation engines, and dynamical systems. We aim at providing principles, methodologies, and algorithms to address well-established as well as emerging applications from the graph perspective.

Graphs as Data.

Graphs provide a natural data structure to capture dependency between data points. In this line, we have built graph neural networks and other graph learning algorithms that address heterogeneity, generalizability, scalability, and explainability, and successfully applied them to e-commerce, social networks, and healthcare.

Dynamical Prediction

Dynamical System Predictions: Our universe is a dynamical system. Accurately predicting dynamics is a key approach to artificial intelligence. Our goal is to understand how all agents in a dynamic system interact and then predict their future evolution patterns.

Causal Dynamics: Standard dynamic learning inevitably relies on spurious correlations, thus limiting robustness and generalization. To overcome this challenge, we study how to integrate causal models into dynamical system prediction.

Fairness

Fair Machine Learning: Machine learning models can be highly biased when used in decision making, leading to moral discrimination based on gender, sexual orientation, race, etc. We value fairness and aim to design more trustworthy and responsible machine learning models.
